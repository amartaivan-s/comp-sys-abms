Complex systems are everywhere ‚Äî from the movement of pedestrians and markets to ecosystems and social networks. Agent-Based Modeling and Simulation (ABMS) provides a computational microscope for understanding how simple individual actions lead to emergent, large-scale phenomena.

What Are Complex Systems?

A complex system consists of many interacting parts (agents) whose collective behavior cannot be simply inferred from the properties of the parts themselves. These systems exhibit emergence, adaptation, and nonlinearity, making them distinct from merely complicated or mechanistic systems.

‚ÄúMore is different.‚Äù ‚Äî P. W. Anderson (1972)

The study of complex systems seeks to understand how order, patterns, and intelligence emerge spontaneously through local interactions and feedback loops. Such systems exist across disciplines: biology, economics, sociology, physics, and computer science.

Key properties of complex systems include:

  • Nonlinearity ‚Äî small changes can lead to large effects.
  • Feedback loops ‚Äî mutual influence and co-adaptation among components.
  • Emergence ‚Äî macroscopic patterns arising without centralized control.
  • Self-organization ‚Äî spontaneous formation of structures or behaviors.
  • Adaptivity ‚Äî agents learn, evolve, or respond to changing environments.

Examples of Complex Systems

  • Social systems: opinion dynamics, cooperation, cultural diffusion.
  • Economic systems: market fluctuations, consumer behavior, supply networks.
  • Biological systems: ecosystems, immune networks, collective animal behavior.
  • Urban and mobility systems: pedestrian flow, traffic dynamics, city growth.
  • Technological systems: distributed robotics, Internet, AI agent ecosystems.
